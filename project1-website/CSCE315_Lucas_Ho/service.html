Service

Epic Movement: A student run ministry where the goal of the organization is to reach Asian American Pacific Islanders and tell them about Jesus. Weekly events include bible studies, large group gatherings, community events, and outreach events where the organization goes out and talks to people on campus to get to know the students better. This is an organization that is under Cru, which is another on campus ministry and it aims to help people learn who Jesus is and help them follow Him. Ultimately, the goal of Epic is to build relationships with the student body and also invite them to our events.

Concessions: Concessions is way to help Texas A&M and support their football team. Concessions is an event where people, clubs, and organizations can help run food stands at Kyle Field during football games. With so many people there is going to be a great demand for food, which takes a lot of people to run the stands and give people food. Concessions is a full day event where people will either work the register, prepare food in the back, or even offer cool beverages to the spectators of the football games.

Big Event: Big Event is a one day community service event where Aggies volunteer to serve the community of Bryan and College Station. It is a student-run project where Aggies are placed in teams to complete service projects for residents of the community such as yard work, gardening, or window washing. Big event was introduced in 1982, so it has been continuing for 41 years. This event happens every spring and thousands of Aggies participate in showing appreciation to families of the College Station and Bryan area. This is the largest one day student run service project in the nation, and the mission is to say thank you to the community.
